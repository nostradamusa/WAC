# World Albanian Congress — Product Vision

## Mission

The World Albanian Congress (WAC) platform exists to connect Albanians across the globe into a coordinated professional, economic, and cultural network.

The goal is to transform a fragmented diaspora into a structured ecosystem where individuals, businesses, organizations, and opportunities can find each other and collaborate.

WAC is not designed as a typical social network. It is intended to function as a **diaspora infrastructure platform**.

The platform should help Albanians:

- find each other professionally
- hire within the community
- mentor the next generation
- discover Albanian-owned businesses
- organize community efforts
- invest together
- support leadership development
- coordinate across countries

The long-term objective is to strengthen the Albanian diaspora economically, professionally, and culturally.

---

# Target Users

Primary user segments include:

### Professionals
Engineers, doctors, lawyers, sales professionals, entrepreneurs, academics, and specialists across industries.

### Business Owners
Owners of restaurants, construction companies, agencies, startups, consulting firms, and other businesses.

### Organizations
Student associations, nonprofit groups, chambers of commerce, religious organizations, and cultural institutions.

### Young Professionals and Students
Members of the diaspora who are early in their careers and looking for mentorship and opportunity.

### Community Leaders
Individuals organizing events, initiatives, and community projects.

---

# Core Value Proposition

The platform must make the following possible:

1. Discover Albanians in any profession or industry.

2. Locate Albanian-owned businesses in any region.

3. Connect with mentors, collaborators, or investors.

4. Organize events and initiatives within the diaspora.

5. Coordinate opportunities such as jobs, partnerships, and referrals.

The guiding principle is:

**Make it easy for Albanians to find and help each other.**

---

# Platform Philosophy

The platform should prioritize:

- structured data
- discoverability
- trust
- community value

Avoid building a typical social media feed.

Instead focus on:

- directories
- relationships
- opportunities
- introductions

The platform should feel closer to a **professional network and ecosystem directory** than a traditional social platform.

---

# Core Platform Entities

The system revolves around three primary entity types.

### People

Individual members of the diaspora.

Profiles include professional information, skills, location, and opportunities they are open to.

Examples:

- pharmaceutical sales representative
- software engineer
- lawyer
- entrepreneur

---

### Businesses

Companies owned or operated by Albanians.

Examples:

- restaurants
- construction companies
- recruiting agencies
- consulting firms
- technology startups

Businesses may hire, sponsor events, or offer services to the community.

---

### Organizations

Community organizations, nonprofits, student groups, and cultural institutions.

Examples:

- Albanian student associations
- nonprofit organizations
- chambers of commerce
- cultural groups

Organizations help coordinate events and initiatives.

---

# Core Platform Features

## Member Profiles

Each user has a structured professional profile.

Profiles include:

- name
- headline
- profession
- company
- industry
- specialty
- skills
- location
- ancestry roots
- open-to flags

Profiles are designed for discovery.

---

## People Directory

The People Directory allows users to search and filter members by:

- location
- industry
- profession
- skills
- openness to mentorship or hiring

The directory is the primary discovery engine.

---

## Business Directory

The platform will allow Albanian-owned businesses to create listings.

Users will be able to search for businesses by:

- location
- industry
- services offered

This strengthens economic connections within the diaspora.

---

## Organizations

Community organizations can create profiles and publish:

- events
- initiatives
- announcements

This helps coordinate community activity.

---

## Ask the Network

Users can post requests such as:

- job opportunities
- hiring requests
- mentorship requests
- referrals
- professional advice

This feature drives engagement and collaboration.

---

# Long-Term Vision

The long-term vision is to develop the platform into a global infrastructure for the Albanian diaspora.

Future expansions may include:

- investment networks
- venture capital collaboration
- diaspora business development
- leadership programs
- mentorship networks
- political coordination
- nonprofit collaboration

The platform should scale to support **hundreds of thousands of members globally**.

---

# Guiding Product Principles

1. Prioritize structured data over unstructured content.

2. Focus on discoverability and connection.

3. Keep onboarding simple.

4. Build tools that create real-world opportunities.

5. Design for long-term scalability.

---

# Success Criteria

The platform is successful if users can easily:

- find professionals in their field
- locate businesses within the community
- build relationships across the diaspora
- collaborate on opportunities

The platform should become the **central coordination hub for Albanians worldwide**.
