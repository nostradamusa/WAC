Our Purpose
Why the World Albanian Congress Exists

Albanian organizations across the world often operate in isolation — powerful within their own communities, but siloed from each other. A cultural center in the Bronx and a business association in Chicago may never speak. A diaspora network in London may not know what advocacy is happening in Washington.

The World Albanian Congress bridges these gaps — creating communication, coordination, and collective power across our entire global community.

Who We Are

About the World Albanian Congress

🦅

A Congress for All Albanians, Everywhere

The World Albanian Congress exists to connect, amplify, and protect the Albanian people and their culture across every country, every community, and every generation.

Albanian organizations — cultural centers, business associations, legal aid networks, student alliances — often operate in isolation. They are powerful within their own domains but rarely communicate with each other. Events go uncoordinated. Resources go uncombined. The Albanian voice on the global stage goes amplified only in pieces.

The World Albanian Congress changes that. We are the connective tissue of the Albanian diaspora — a neutral convening body that brings organizational leaders together, shares resources and intelligence across the network, and presents a unified front when advocacy is needed.

We are especially proud of our partnership with the Albanian American Cultural Center of Riverdale (Struga) — the largest Albanian community center in America and the spiritual heart of the diaspora's first and second generations.

College Prep & Future Planning
for Albanian Teens

Whether you're aiming for a 4-year university, a trade school, or exploring your options — we have mentors, timelines, and resources to help you get there with your Albanian identity front and center.

Your College Roadmap
9
Freshman Year — Build Your Foundation

Focus on grades and join at least 2 extracurriculars. Start exploring what subjects interest you. Attend a WAC college info session to understand the landscape early.

10
Sophomore Year — Explore & Test

Take a PSAT for practice. Research colleges online. Consider a WAC-sponsored job shadow with an Albanian professional in your field of interest.

11
Junior Year — Prepare Seriously

Take the SAT/ACT (aim for 2 attempts). Build your college list: 3 reach, 4 match, 3 safety. Request a meeting with our college advisor through WAC. Start your Common App essay draft.

12
Senior Year — Apply & Commit

Submit early decision/early action by November. Apply for FAFSA as soon as October 1. Apply for the WAC Albanian Heritage Scholarship. Commit to your school by May 1.
